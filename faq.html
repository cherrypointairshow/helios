Frequently Asked Questions

  • How much does it cost to attend?

    There's no charge! Both attendance and parking are FREE. Cherry Point hosts the show as an open house to enhance community relations, show off the latest technology that our military has to offer and also serves as a recruiting tool. You can however, purchase preferred seating in the form of bleachers, box seats & pavilion seating and of course, you'll want a keepsake in the form of a T-shirt, sweatshirt or ball cap. If hunger or thirst sets in, we'll have an enormous variety of food and drink on hand. Plus, there'll be aviation novelties and collectibles galore.

  • Do you have any parking tips?

    Come early - We expect thousands of attendees each day, but with the size of the tarmac, it shouldn't seem too crowded no matter the amount of people.

    Come early - We expect thousands of attendees each day, but with the size of the tarmac, it shouldn't seem too crowded no matter the amount of people.

    Shuttle Buses - The Air Show will be providing a series of buses for guests to enjoy throughout the day. Be sure to note the area where your car's parked to alleviate any confusion when your day ends.

    Special Needs parking - IF YOU HAVE A STATE ISSUED HANDICAP PARKING PRIVILEDGE, PLEASE ENSURE IT IS DISPLAYED AND ENTER THE AIR SHOW VIA THE MAIN GATE ON HIGHWAY 101. Follow the signs designated for Special Needs, Handicapped and Disabled parking area. There will be transportation provided to those in wheelchairs or those with special needs to exclusive seating areas. Due to limited transportation and seating, only 1 person may accompany the special needs guest. ALSO, because of space limitations IN THESE SPECIAL SEATING AREAS, we encourage you to arrive early

  • We have small children will there be anything for them to do?

    Lots! The Air Show will have several displays, events and rides for the kiddies to enjoy. A variety of inflatable's, aviation related fun and various games will highlight junior's day.

  • Besides lots of aerial performers, what else is on tap?

    There'll be lots to enjoy both in the air and on the ground. You'll see numerous static displays from supersonic jets to military vintage bombers, with numerous booths, lots of activities for the youngsters and great variety of food and drink.

  • Is there parking for privately owned aircraft aboard Cherry Point?

    No. We wish we could accommodate but we can't because there is not sufficient ramp space to park privately owned aircraft. Eastern Carolina Regional Airport in New Bern, NC is only a short 20 minute drive and is the closest airport to Cherry Point.

  • How can my business be involved with the Air Show?

    We have numerous options for local businesses and corporations to highlight their wares and skills. From a booth , to VIP chalets, or a corporate partnership involving thousand of impressions and a great return on your investment, you can't go wrong with eastern NC's largest spectator event. To contact our corporate relations' staff at 466-6847.

  • Where is the air show located?

    The 2014 Air Show will be held aboard the east coast's premiere Marine Corps jet base, MCAS Cherry Point, centrally located in Havelock, NC. Close to numerous cities and towns, the air show is a short drive from anywhere in eastern NC for a day of FREE family fun. See our directions page for more information.

  • Additional tips

    Bring a lawn chair or blanket, hearing protection, sunburn protection, earplugs and a visor or cap to keep the sun and wind under control. Just in case you forget something, we'll have it available for purchase at the show.

Additional security information

All individuals entering the flightline area through the admission gate are subject to search.

Please come early, pay careful attention to all signs and announcements, and be patient. These procedures are in place to ensure the safety of those attending the Air Show.

When arriving aboard the Air Station, we ask that you pay strict attention to road signs and directions by Military and Civilian law enforcement personnel.

There will be designated entry points to enter the flightline. These will be the only points that spectators will be allowed to enter. Please for your safety and ours do not try and enter from any other point.

Security

Prohibited items

  • Firearms

    Weapons (regardless of permit; either on a person or in a vehicle) including firearms, knives (including pocket knives and multi-tools), replica or toy weapons, pepper spray/mace, throwing stars, nun-chucks or stun guns. Anyone found with a firearm will be immediately apprehended and turned over to local authorities.

  • Walking sticks

    Only exception is handicap use.

  • Transmitters or walkie-talkies of any kind

    For security and safety reasons you will be asked to return these items to your vehicle prior to entry.

  • Containers

    Coolers, picnic bags/baskets, backpacks, duffle bags or briefcases.

  • Pets

    Only guide dogs are allowed.

  • Other items

    Scanners, bicycles, skateboards, scooters, skates. You will be asked to return any of these items to your vehicle prior to entry.

Approved items

  • Pagers/Cell Phones

    You might be asked to turn them on and off to validate their authenticity.

  • Cameras

    Please do not load them with film. Security personnel might ask you to open them. Digital cameras might have to be turned off and on to validate their authenticity. Film can be loaded after clearing security.

  • Video Camera

    Security personnel might ask you to open them to validate their authenticity.

  • Camera Bags

    Small camera bags will be allowed, but might be searched.

  • Diaper Bags, Baby Strollers, Wheel Chairs and Lawn Chairs

    All items might be searched.

  • Medications

    Should you be a diabetic, have an allergic reaction to bee stings or have a medical condition that requires you to carry a needle to administer medication, you will be allowed to enter the grounds to the air show, however we ask that you disclose this information upon your arrival and have proper equipment for disposal of used needles. In addition, all roving Emergency Medical Specialist and First Aid Stations will have Sharps containers for disposal of any needles or medical waste.

Notice

If you elect not to return these items to your vehicle and choose to leave them at the security check point we will not be responsible for the return of the item.

These measures are being taken to ensure everyone's safety.
